HOW TO USE THIS JOURNAL

Welcome to The 90-Day Wine Tasting Journal — your personal companion for
every glass, every vineyard, and every wonderful sip along the way.
Whether you're a weekend wine lover who enjoys tasting at local wineries
or someone who simply wants to remember which bottle you loved at dinner
last Saturday, this journal was made for you.

This journal is designed to help you build a beautiful, personalized
record of your wine journey over the next 90 days. You don't need to be
a sommelier or know the difference between a Bordeaux and a Burgundy to
get started — all you need is a glass of something you enjoy and a few
minutes to jot down your impressions.

QUICK-LOG TASTING PAGES (Pages 5–111)

The heart of this journal, these 107 pages give you a structured but
relaxed format for logging each wine you try. Fill in the wine name,
winery, vintage, varietal, and region at the top. Rate the wine using
the five boxes — one box for "not for me," five for "absolutely
reordering this." Note what you see (color, clarity), what you smell
(aromas), and what you taste (palate) — even simple words like "cherry,"
"oak," or "crisp" are perfect. Check off flavors in the Flavor Profile
section to help you discover your palate preferences over time. Use the
Finish section to describe how long the flavors last after you swallow.
Jot any final thoughts in Overall Notes, and mark whether you'd buy it
again. Over 90 days, these pages will reveal patterns in what you love —
and what you'd rather skip.

VINEYARD VISIT & WISH LIST PAGES (Pages 112–141)

Planning a trip to wine country? Use these pages before you go to jot
down vineyards you're dreaming of visiting. After each visit, capture the
vineyard name, location, atmosphere, the wines you tasted, and any
bottles you brought home. The Wish List section at the bottom of each
page lets you note which vineyards to explore next. These pages become a
wonderful travel diary of your wine adventures.

REGIONAL GRAPE VARIETY INDEX (Pages 142–149)

These eight pages are your personal reference guide. As you discover new
grape varieties and wine regions, record them here with key flavor notes
and your personal ratings. Over time, this becomes your own curated wine
reference that no book or app can replicate — because it's based entirely
on what you've actually tasted. Use the Region / Appellation field at
the top of each page to organize your index by area.

HOW TO TASTE WINE — A QUICK GUIDE

Look: Hold your glass against a white background and note the color and
clarity. A rich ruby red, a pale straw gold, a deep garnet — these clues
hint at the grape variety and age.

Swirl: Gently swirl the wine to release its aromas. Don't worry about
technique — a few slow circles in the glass is all it takes.

Smell: Dip your nose into the glass and breathe in slowly. What do you
notice first? Fruit? Flowers? Something earthy or spicy? Trust your
instincts — there are no wrong answers.

Taste: Take a small sip and let the wine coat your mouth. Notice the
flavors, the weight (light, medium, or full-bodied), and the texture
(smooth, tannic, or crisp).

Finish: After you swallow, pay attention to the lingering flavors. A
long, pleasant finish is often the mark of a quality wine.

Tips for Getting the Most Out of This Journal: Try to fill in your
tasting notes while the wine is still in your glass — your impressions
will be sharper. Don't worry about using "correct" wine language; your
own words are the most meaningful. Look back at your entries every few
weeks to spot patterns — you may discover you have a strong preference
for Pinot Noirs or that you always prefer wines under $20. By the end of
90 days, you'll have a personal wine profile that makes every future
purchase smarter and more enjoyable.
