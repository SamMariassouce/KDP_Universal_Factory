PAGES 3-4 — HOW TO USE THIS BOOK (2 pages)
(Copy and paste into Affinity Publisher)
-------------------------------------
Welcome to The Elegant Wine Tasting Journal — your personal companion
for savoring, remembering, and sharing the wines that matter most to you.

Whether you enjoy a glass at home, gather with your wine club, or explore
tasting rooms on a vineyard tour, this journal is designed to help you
capture every beautiful moment along the way. There is no right or wrong
way to use it. All you need is a glass in hand and a little curiosity.

Here is how each section works:

WINE LABEL COLLECTION PAGES (pages 5-24)
These pages are made for your inner curator. When you open a bottle you
love — or even one that surprises you — peel off the label and tape or
glue it right into the large bordered space on the page. Below the label,
you will find fields to jot down the key details: wine name, vintage, the
date you tasted it, the region, the producer, how much it cost, and a
quick personal rating out of five. There is also a generous notes section
where you can capture anything that stood out — a funny moment, a perfect
food match, or simply why you would buy it again. These pages become a
beautiful visual record of your wine journey over time.

TASTING NOTES PAGES (pages 25-114)
This is the heart of the journal — ninety pages for recording detailed
impressions of the wines you try. Each log guides you through the full
experience: who you shared the bottle with, the occasion or setting, what
the wine looked like, how it smelled, how it tasted across key dimensions
like sweetness, acidity, tannin, and body, and how long the finish lasted.
At the bottom, you rate the wine overall and write your final impression
in your own words. These pages are perfect for evenings with friends, wine
club nights, or any time a wine makes you want to remember it.

WINE AND FOOD PAIRING IDEAS (pages 115-119)
Great wine is even better with great food. Use these pages to collect your
favorite pairing discoveries as you go. Write the wine in the left column
and the foods that worked beautifully with it in the right column. Over
time, you will build your own personal pairing guide — tailored entirely
to your palate, not a textbook's.

A few tips to get the most from this journal:

Try to write your notes while the wine is still in the glass. Your first
impressions are often the most honest. Do not worry about using the
"right" words — describe what you actually smell and taste, even if it is
something unexpected like pencil shavings, cherry candy, or ocean air.
Those personal associations are what make tasting notes memorable.

Share this journal at your next wine gathering and invite friends to add
their notes alongside yours. Wine is always better in good company, and
seeing different people's impressions of the same bottle can be surprising
and delightful.

This journal was designed for women who appreciate the finer things —
beautiful moments, meaningful connections, and wines worth remembering.
Enjoy every pour.
