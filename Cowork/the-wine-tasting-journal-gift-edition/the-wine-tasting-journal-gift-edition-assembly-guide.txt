HOW TO USE THIS BOOK

Whether this journal was a gift for a birthday, a wedding, an anniversary, or simply a celebration of someone you love who loves wine, it was designed to become a beautiful record of their wine journey — one glass at a time.

WHO THIS BOOK IS FOR

This journal is for wine lovers who appreciate the social and emotional side of wine just as much as the taste. It is for the person who wants to remember the bottle shared at a milestone dinner, the wine discovered on a holiday, or the special label brought out to celebrate something meaningful. You do not need technical knowledge to use this book — just a willingness to pay attention and a love of good wine and good company.

YOUR THREE SECTION TYPES

This journal contains three different page types, each designed for a different kind of recording.

The Standard Tasting Log is your everyday companion. Use it for any wine you open — whether it's a Tuesday night pour, a restaurant bottle, or something new from a local shop. It walks you through the key things to notice: how the wine looks, how it smells, how it tastes, how long the finish lingers, and whether you'd buy it again. Over time, these pages will become a personal record of your taste preferences, your discoveries, and the wines that moved you.

The Milestone Tasting Page is reserved for the wines that mark an occasion. These pages have space for the celebration, the people you shared it with, what made the wine special, and a memory to hold onto. Use one whenever you open a bottle for a birthday dinner, a toast at a wedding, a holiday gathering, or any moment you want to remember. These are the pages that make this journal a keepsake.

The Wines to Try Wishlist is your running list of bottles to track down. Each time someone recommends a wine, you see one on a menu that intrigues you, or you read about a producer you want to explore, add it here. Mark the priority level and check it off when you finally try it. This section keeps the adventure going forward.

HOW TO GET THE MOST FROM THIS JOURNAL

You do not need to fill in every single field on every page. The tasting logs are designed to guide you, not test you — use as much or as little detail as feels natural for each wine. Over time, as you fill more pages, you will start to notice patterns in what you enjoy: whether you lean toward lighter or fuller wines, aromatic or subtle noses, high acidity or soft tannins. That self-knowledge is one of the most rewarding things a tasting journal can give you.

For the milestone pages, write while the memory is still fresh. The details that feel obvious in the moment — who was there, what was said, why that bottle felt right — are exactly what you will treasure when you look back years from now.

And whenever a wine surprises you, disappoints you, or makes you want to track down a dozen more bottles of the same, reach for this journal. It is yours to fill exactly as you wish.
