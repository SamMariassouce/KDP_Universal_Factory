How to Use This Journal

The 90-Day Tasting Wine Journal is designed for the serious wine
enthusiast who wants to move beyond casual drinking and build a
disciplined, analytical approach to experiencing wine. Whether you
are managing an active cellar, developing your palate for WSET or
sommelier study, or simply determined to deepen your knowledge over
the next 90 days, this journal gives you the structure to do it
properly.

Your journal contains four types of pages, each serving a distinct
purpose in building your wine knowledge and collection records.

The Wine Cellar Inventory Log pages (pages 4-18) are your cellar's
permanent record. Use them to document every bottle you own — its
producer, vintage, varietal, storage location, quantity on hand,
and purchase price. Update these logs whenever you add or remove
bottles from your cellar. A well-maintained inventory protects
your investment and ensures you always know exactly what you have
and where it is.

The Detailed Tasting Template pages (pages 19-158) are the heart
of this journal. Use one page for every wine you evaluate. Work
through each section methodically: begin with Sight to assess
color, depth, and clarity; move to Nose to identify aromas and
intensity; then evaluate the Palate for body, tannins, acidity,
and finish; and conclude with your overall rating and food pairing
notes. Writing in complete sentences — not just checked boxes —
will sharpen your tasting vocabulary far more quickly than any
other method.

The Vintage Comparison Chart pages (pages 159-168) are for
vertical tasting analysis. When you taste multiple vintages of
the same wine, use one chart per wine to record how each year
differs in color, aromas, body, tannin structure, acidity, and
finish. Over time, these charts will reveal how a producer's
style evolves and which vintages are peaking or declining.

The Monthly Acquisition and Collection Progress Log pages
(pages 169-182) give you a monthly financial and inventory
snapshot. Record every bottle you acquire — including quantity,
price per bottle, total spend, and storage location — and track
your running collection total. Use the Notes section to reflect
on your purchasing decisions, budget adherence, and any emerging
gaps in your cellar.

To get the most from this journal, commit to completing a tasting
entry for every wine you open, regardless of how casual the
occasion. The collectors who develop the most refined palates are
those who record consistently, not just when the wine feels
significant. Aim for at least one detailed tasting entry every
two days over the 90-day period — your future self will thank you
for the discipline.
